Carers Week

Brighton & Hove Celebrates Carers Week 2026: Building Carer Friendly Communities

Carers Week 2026 (8–14 June) kicks off this weekend, celebrating the vital work of unpaid carers supporting family members and friends who have a disability, mental health condition, or physical illness. Brighton & Hove City Council has reaffirmed its commitment to making the city a Carer Friendly City, with events including a Carers Festival at the Open Market on 10 June and a stall at the Rotary Family Festival at Hove Park on 7 June. Mind in Brighton and Hove will host a mental health and wellbeing stall at the Carers Festival.

SEND Reform

Brighton & Hove SEND Reform Plan Due for Submission on 19 June

Brighton & Hove City Council is in the final stages of completing its SEND Reform Plan, which will be submitted to the Department for Education on 19 June 2026. The plan focuses on strengthening mainstream support, developing an 'Experts at Hand' model giving schools consistent access to specialists, earlier identification of needs, and reducing the distances children travel for provision.

Equality

Council Reports Progress on Disability Pay Gap — Now Just -0.3%

Brighton & Hove City Council's latest workforce equalities report reveals the median disability pay gap has shifted to -0.3%, meaning disabled employees now earn marginally more than non-disabled colleagues on average. This marks a significant improvement from the 5.8% gap reported in 2023–24, and disabled staff representation in the upper pay band rose from 7.6% to 9.7%.

Arts & Fundraising

Amaze's Story Trail: 30+ BookBench Sculptures Coming to Brighton This Autumn

Disability charity Amaze has partnered with Wild in Art to bring The Story Trail to Brighton & Hove from September to November 2026. Over 30 giant BookBench sculptures designed by local artists will be displayed across the city's streets and parks for eight weeks in a free, family-friendly accessible art trail. Amaze is actively seeking volunteers and sponsors, with sculptures to be auctioned afterwards to raise funds for SEND families — 1 in 6 Sussex families support a child with a disability.

Accessibility

Beach Wheelchair Service Paused as City's Accessibility Guide Updated

Brighton & Hove Council's free beach wheelchair loan service remains paused as of early 2026, prompting a new practical accessibility guide for the city. The guide highlights that the seafront promenade is fully accessible, buses have ramps and talking stop announcements, and newer venues like Sea Lanes were designed with access built in. However, The Lanes, steep hills in Hanover and Kemptown, and many historic buildings remain challenging for wheelchair users.

Brighton Insider · March 2026

SEND Charity

Amaze Now Hosts All Three Sussex Parent Carer Forums

From April 2026, Amaze officially took on hosting the West Sussex Parent Carer Forum, adding to its existing hosting of PaCC (Brighton & Hove) and ESPCF (East Sussex). The three forums have formed the Sussex PCF Collaborative to speak with a unified voice amid changes in SEND policy and local governance. Each forum retains its own identity and decision-making while Amaze provides back-office support.
